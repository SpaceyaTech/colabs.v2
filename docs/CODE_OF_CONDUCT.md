# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of Colabs pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Crediting others for their contributions
- Helping newcomers find their footing in the project

### Examples of unacceptable behaviour

- The use of sexualised language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (physical or email address) without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions, pull requests, issues, or community spaces
- Dismissing or belittling contributions from others, regardless of experience level
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within **all community spaces** managed by the Colabs project, including:

- This GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, comments, code reviews)
- Our [Discord server](https://discord.gg/UKjtBDDFHH)
- Our social media accounts ([@SpaceYaTech](https://x.com/SpaceYaTech) on X, Instagram, LinkedIn, and TikTok)
- Any other channels or events where a community member is officially representing Colabs

It also applies when an individual is **officially representing the community** in public spaces — for example, using a project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or in-person event.

## Reporting an Incident

If you witness or experience behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it. All reports are taken seriously, reviewed promptly, and handled with confidentiality.

### How to report

| Channel                                                       | When to use                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 📧 **Email**: [conduct@colabs.dev](mailto:conduct@colabs.dev) | Formal reports; private and confidential                 |
| 💬 **Discord DM** to a moderator                              | Urgent or real-time situations within the Discord server |

When reporting, please include:

- Your contact information (if you are comfortable sharing it)
- A description of the incident, including date, time, and location (channel, issue, etc.)
- Names or usernames of those involved
- Any relevant screenshots, links, or other evidence
- Whether you believe the incident is ongoing

The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident. We will **never** publicly disclose a reporter's identity without explicit consent.

## Enforcement

### Our commitment

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face repercussions as determined by other members of the project leadership.

### Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will follow these graduated steps when determining the appropriate response to a violation. The severity of the response will match the severity and pattern of the behaviour.

#### 1. Correction

**Community impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behaviour deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from the maintainers that provides clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behaviour was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

---

#### 2. Warning

**Community impact:** A violation through a single incident or a series of related actions.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with clear consequences for continued behaviour. The person is asked to have no unsolicited interaction with the people involved, including in community spaces like Discord and GitHub, for a specified period. Violating these terms may result in a temporary or permanent ban.

---

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from all community interaction and public communication channels for a specified period. This includes GitHub (issues, PRs, discussions) and the Discord server. No public or private interaction with the people involved is permitted during this period. Violating these terms may result in a permanent ban.

---

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community impact:** Demonstrating a clear pattern of violation — including sustained harassment, targeted aggression toward an individual or group, or an escalating series of lesser violations.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from all forms of community participation, including the GitHub repository, Discord server, and all SpaceYaTech-managed spaces.
